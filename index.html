Bio

I received the B.Eng. degree in information engineering from Southeast University, Nanjing, China, in 2014, under the supervision of Prof. Wei Xu. In Aug. 2018, I received the Ph.D. degree in electronic and computer engineering (ECE) from the Hong Kong University of Science and Technology (HKUST), Kowloon, Hong Kong, under the supervision of Prof. Khaled B. LETAIEF and Prof. Jun ZHANG. Currently, I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Institute for Digital Communications (IDC) at Friedrich-Alexander-Universität Erlangen-Nürnberg (FAU), Erlangen, Germany. I am supported by the prestigious Humboldt Research Fellowship, hosted by Prof. Robert Schober. I was the recipient of the IEEE GLOBECOM 2017 Best Paper Award and The 2018 IEEE Signal Processing Society Young Author Best Paper Award. I am also a co-author of the book Stochastic Geometry Analysis of Multi-Antenna Wireless Networks (Springer, 2019). My research interests include millimeter wave communications, MIMO systems, mathematical optimization, large intelligent surface-assisted communications, and stochastic geometry.
